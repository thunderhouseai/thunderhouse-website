# Voice and Tone — ThunderHouse

## Brand Voice

ThunderHouse sounds like a **smart, experienced colleague who happens to be an expert in AI implementation**. Not a vendor. Not a salesperson. Not a tech bro. A trusted professional who speaks plainly and backs it up with results.

### Core voice traits:

**Direct.** We say what we mean. "You're losing clients in 4 places" is better than "there may be opportunities to improve client acquisition."

**Clear.** We explain AI in business terms: patients, leads, appointments, documents — not APIs, models, or algorithms.

**Confident without arrogance.** We know this works. We don't hedge everything with "might" and "could." We also don't oversell.

**Professional without stiffness.** We sound like a real human being. Not a corporate press release. Not a startup hype machine.

**Diagnostic.** We ask questions and name problems before proposing solutions. This comes through in copy as well: describe the problem accurately before introducing the solution.

## What to Avoid

**Hype language:**
- "Cutting-edge" / "Next-generation" / "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing"
- "Transform your business" (vague)
- "The power of AI" (meaningless without context)
- "Scale your growth" (generic agency speak)

**Vague promises:**
- "Increase revenue" (without specifics)
- "Save time" (without saying how much or how)
- "Streamline your workflow" (sounds like nothing)

**Tech jargon:**
- "Machine learning pipeline"
- "Large language models"
- "Natural language processing"
- "API integrations" (say "connect your tools" instead)
- "Omnichannel communication" (say "SMS, WhatsApp, and email" instead)

**Fake urgency:**
- "Limited slots available"
- "Act now"
- "Don't miss out"

**Corporate stiffness:**
- "We are pleased to offer..."
- "Our solutions empower..."
- "Leveraging cutting-edge technology to..."

## Writing Rules

1. **Use the word "you" and "your" freely.** The reader is the subject. ThunderHouse is the helper.

2. **Sentence length:** Mix short and medium. Short sentences for emphasis. Medium for explanation. Never long and complex.

3. **Active voice always.** "We build the system" not "The system is built by us."

4. **Specific over general.** "Patient appointment reminders via SMS and WhatsApp" beats "automated communication solutions."

5. **Lead with the problem.** Every major section should open by naming the problem before the solution.

6. **Say what you do, not what you are.** "We build intake systems that..." is better than "ThunderHouse is an innovative AI firm that..."

7. **End CTAs with action verbs.** "Get Your Free Audit" / "Request My Audit" / "See How It Works." Not "Learn more" or "Click here."

8. **Do not explain technology unless asked.** The buyer doesn't need to understand how n8n or Anthropic work. They need to understand what changes on Monday morning.

## Spanish Tone Notes

- Natural Puerto Rico business Spanish — not formal Castilian, not literal translation
- Acceptable anglicisms: email, WhatsApp, follow-up, intake, workflow, dashboard, app, SMS
- Use informal "tú" (not "usted") for all copy — matches the warmer, more direct communication style of PR business culture
- Match the directness of the English copy — don't over-soften for Spanish
- Avoid robotic structure — if it sounds like it was translated by software, rewrite it
