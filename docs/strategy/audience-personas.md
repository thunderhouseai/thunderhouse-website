# Audience Personas — ThunderHouse

## Overview

ThunderHouse targets owners and practice managers of professional service businesses. These are high-trust, relationship-driven businesses where the owner is often the primary decision-maker and the most time-constrained person in the organization.

---

## Persona 1: Dr. Martínez — Medical Practice Owner

**Demographics**
- Age: 38–58
- Location: Puerto Rico (San Juan, Bayamón, Ponce) or Orlando metro
- Practice type: Primary care, specialty clinic, dental, or therapy practice
- Staff: 2–15 people
- Revenue: $300K–$2M/year

**Daily Reality**
- Sees 15–30 patients per day
- Has limited time for anything administrative
- Relies on front desk staff to handle scheduling, reminders, and patient intake
- Frequently hears: "We missed 3 patients today because they didn't show up and nobody confirmed"
- Has a high no-show rate (10–25%) that costs real revenue
- Patients fill out paper forms or emailed PDFs that staff re-enter manually
- After-hours inquiries are missed until the next business day

**What They Actually Want**
- Fewer no-shows without making staff spend hours calling
- New patient inquiries answered quickly
- Less time spent on paperwork and reminders
- Staff freed to focus on patient care, not phone tag

**What Makes Them Skeptical**
- Has tried EHR systems that promised automation and required months of setup
- Worried about HIPAA and patient data
- Doesn't trust "tech people" who don't understand healthcare
- Budget is tight — needs to see ROI before committing

**What Builds Trust**
- Explaining things in patient and practice terms (not "API integrations")
- Acknowledging HIPAA and explaining how data is handled simply
- Showing that this works specifically for medical practices
- Offering the audit at no cost and no commitment
- Speaking Spanish comfortably if they prefer it

**What Triggers Action**
- A no-show week that cost several hundred dollars in lost appointments
- Losing a patient inquiry because nobody followed up over the weekend
- Staff complaining about how much time they spend on reminders
- Seeing a competitor's practice running smoothly

**Common Objections**
- "We already have an EHR system"
- "I don't want my patients' data in some AI thing"
- "My front desk does fine"
- "How much does this cost?"

## Persona 2: Lcdo. Rivera — Attorney / Law Firm Partner

**Demographics**
- Age: 35–55
- Location: Puerto Rico (San Juan, Guaynabo) or Orlando
- Practice: 1–3 partner firm or solo practitioner
- Practice areas: Family law, criminal defense, personal injury, real estate, immigration, employment
- Staff: 1–6 people
- Revenue: $150K–$800K/year

**Daily Reality**
- Spends most of the day in client work, court, or research
- Relies on a paralegal or legal assistant to handle intake and scheduling
- Gets inquiries via website, Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, and phone — all handled inconsistently
- Leads that come in after hours or on weekends often go cold before Monday
- Scheduling consultations requires multiple back-and-forth emails or calls
- Document collection before consultations is a recurring headache
- Has lost cases to competitors who responded faster

**What They Actually Want**
- Leads followed up immediately, even after hours
- Consultations booked without coordinator involvement
- Documents collected before the first meeting
- Less time on admin, more time billable

**What Makes Them Skeptical**
- Has gotten burned by marketing agencies that promised leads and delivered nothing
- Concerned about attorney-client privilege and confidentiality
- Doesn't believe "AI" can handle the nuance of legal inquiry
- Has seen technology promises that didn't deliver

**What Builds Trust**
- Explaining that automation handles contact info and scheduling — not case details
- Demonstrating with a concrete example: "Someone fills your form Friday night. Monday morning they've hired someone else. Here's what we do instead."
- Showing real results from similar-sized firms
- The free audit removes the risk of trying it

**What Triggers Action**
- A specific incident where a good case went to a competitor
- Realizing they're getting inquiries but not converting them
- Hiring a paralegal specifically to do admin, then watching them do coordination instead of legal work

**Common Objections**
- "I don't want AI handling client communication"
- "What about confidentiality?"
- "Our assistant handles this fine"
- "We already have a CRM"

## Persona 3: CPA Soto — CPA Firm Principal

**Demographics**
- Age: 40–60
- Location: Puerto Rico (Hato Rey, Ponce) or Orlando metro
- Practice type: Individual/small firm CPA, accounting, tax preparation
- Staff: 2–10 people (larger teams during tax season)
- Revenue: $200K–$1.2M/year
- Key clients: Small businesses, individual taxpayers, payroll clients

**Daily Reality**
- Tax season is brutal — staff is already stretched thin
- Spends weeks chasing clients for documents (W-2s, 1099s, financials)
- Clients miss extension deadlines because nobody reminded them
- New client onboarding involves 5–10 emails to collect everything needed
- Status update calls interrupt actual accounting work
- Has occasional staff turnover that creates institutional knowledge gaps

**What They Actually Want**
- Document requests sent and tracked automatically
- Clients reminded about deadlines without manual follow-up
- New client onboarding that doesn't require coordinator involvement
- Client communication that doesn't interrupt the accounting team

**What Makes Them Skeptical**
- Numbers-oriented — wants to see projected ROI before committing
- Has dealt with software that promised to simplify things and created more work
- Concerned about client data security
- Skeptical of "AI" claims — not sure what that means in practice

**What Builds Trust**
- Showing the specific workflow: "Here's how the document reminder sequence works. Here's what the client sees. Here's how your team knows who has responded."
- Speaking practically about time savings (e.g., "You currently spend X hours per client chasing documents. Here's what that looks like after automation.")
- The written audit deliverable appeals to analytical buyers
- Case study from another CPA firm (existing: government portal filing automation)

**What Triggers Action**
- Tax season ending with a team that is exhausted from chasing documents
- A client who filed for an extension that could have been avoided
- Losing a client to a firm that "just made it easier"
- Realizing the same problems happen every single year

**Common Objections**
- "Our clients are older and won't use technology"
- "We already use [software]"
- "I'll think about it after tax season"
- "How secure is client data?"

## Across All Three Personas: Universal Patterns

**What they all share:**
1. They are time-poor professionals, not technology enthusiasts
2. They have seen technology promises not deliver
3. They need to trust the person before trusting the system
4. They want to understand what will change day-to-day for their staff
5. They respond to specific, concrete examples — not abstract benefits
6. Price is secondary to trust and fit

**What the website must do for all three:**
- Speak to their specific problem (not generic "AI for business")
- Explain the solution in their language (patients, cases, clients — not "data flows")
- Remove the risk of engagement (free audit, no commitment)
- Answer: "What will change on Monday morning after you implement this?"
