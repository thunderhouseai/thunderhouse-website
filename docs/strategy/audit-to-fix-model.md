# Audit-to-Fix Model — ThunderHouse

## The Five Phases

### Phase 1: AUDIT
**What happens:** A 30-minute discovery call with the business owner or practice manager.
**What ThunderHouse reviews:**
- How leads, patients, or clients currently contact the business
- What happens immediately after they make contact
- How appointments or consultations are booked
- What follow-up looks like (manual vs. automated)
- Where staff time is spent on repetitive tasks
- Google Business Profile and basic digital presence signals

**What the client needs to bring:** An honest description of their current process. No preparation needed. We ask the questions.

**Client involvement:** 30 minutes on a video call. Low effort, high signal.

---

### Phase 2: DIAGNOSE
**What happens:** ThunderHouse delivers a written summary within 2 business days.
**What the report contains:**
- Specific gaps identified in intake, follow-up, and operations
- A description of the revenue or time cost of each gap (where estimable)
- Ranked recommendations for AI systems to address each gap
- Realistic scope and cost ranges for implementation
- No obligations attached

**Why this phase matters:** The written deliverable differentiates the audit from a sales call. The client gets tangible value before spending a dollar. This builds the trust necessary for a Puerto Rico market buyer to commit.

**Client involvement:** Review the report. Ask questions. No commitment required.

---

### Phase 3: BUILD
**What happens:** ThunderHouse designs and builds the systems identified in the diagnosis.
**Typical build activities:**
- Intake form design and automation logic
- SMS/WhatsApp/email sequence configuration
- Calendar and scheduling flow integration
- Document collection workflow setup
- CRM or tool integrations
- Testing with real scenarios (not just sandbox)

**Client involvement:** Confirmation calls at key milestones. Content/copy approval. Access to existing tools (Google calendar, CRM, etc.).

**Timeline:** 1–6 weeks depending on scope. Typically:
- Simple reminder system: 1–2 weeks
- Full intake automation: 2–3 weeks
- Operations integration: 3–6 weeks

---

### Phase 4: AUTOMATE (Deploy & Train)
**What happens:** Systems go live. Staff is trained. Handoffs are confirmed.
**What this includes:**
- Live deployment with real client data (not a demo)
- Team training session (usually 60–90 minutes)
- Staff Q&A and edge case review
- Monitoring for the first 7–14 days
- Adjustments based on real-world behavior

**Why this phase is critical:** Most technology implementations fail because they're deployed without adoption support. ThunderHouse trains the team and stays until the system is running reliably. This is the "ownership" in end-to-end ownership.

**Client involvement:** Attend the training session. Inform the team of the change. Provide feedback in the first two weeks.

---

### Phase 5: IMPROVE
**What happens:** Ongoing monitoring, measurement, and optimization.
**What ThunderHouse tracks:**
- Sequence open/response rates
- No-show rate trends (for reminder systems)
- Lead response time
- Staff time savings (where measurable)
- System errors or edge cases

**What gets adjusted:**
- Timing and cadence of follow-up messages
- Wording of automated communications
- Routing logic if patterns emerge
- Integration updates when connected tools change

**Client involvement:** Monthly or bi-monthly check-in calls. Review of metrics. Feedback on staff experience.
