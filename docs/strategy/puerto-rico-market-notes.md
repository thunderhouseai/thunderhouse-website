# Puerto Rico Market Notes — ThunderHouse

## Overview

Puerto Rico is ThunderHouse's primary market. Understanding the buyer psychology, business culture, and communication norms of this market is essential for effective website copy, messaging, and service delivery.

---

## Buyer Psychology

**Relationship before transaction.** Puerto Rico is a high-context, relationship-oriented market. Buyers want to know who they are dealing with before they commit to anything. The free audit model is specifically well-suited here — it allows a relationship to begin with no financial risk.

**More explanation needed before trust.** Unlike many US mainland buyers who move faster, Puerto Rican professional service owners typically need more context before they trust a new vendor. This means:
- The website should explain more than it would for a pure US mainland audience
- The "How It Works" section needs to be clear and step-by-step
- The FAQ section on the audit page addresses skepticism head-on
- The positioning should acknowledge that many have been burned by tech vendors before

**Word-of-mouth is the dominant acquisition channel.** Referrals from trusted peers carry disproportionate weight. The website serves primarily to validate and support word-of-mouth, not to cold-convert visitors.

**Price sensitivity with value orientation.** PR professional services firms are price-conscious but will invest in solutions that demonstrably save time or generate revenue. Frame the conversation around time savings and client acquisition, not technology features.

---

## Communication Norms

**Bilingual is expected.** Many Puerto Rico business owners switch between English and Spanish fluidly. The website offers both, and the default should feel natural in both — not like a machine translation.

**Professional but warm.** PR business communication is more personal than a typical mainland US business interaction. Pure corporate formality feels cold. ThunderHouse's tone should be professional but not stiff — like a trusted colleague, not a distant vendor.

**Acknowledge the community.** "We work specifically with Puerto Rican businesses in Puerto Rico and Orlando" is a credibility signal, not just a geographic description. It tells the reader: "These people are from here or understand our world."

**WhatsApp is real.** A significant portion of professional communication in Puerto Rico happens via WhatsApp. When describing automated follow-up systems, WhatsApp is not just an option — it's often the preferred channel for business communication. Always mention it alongside SMS and email.

---

## Common Pain Points Specific to PR Market

**Limited local tech vendors.** Few agencies in Puerto Rico specialize in AI implementation for professional services. Buyers often feel they have to hire mainland US firms who don't understand the market, or DIY with tools they don't have time to learn.

**Staff retention challenges.** Finding and retaining good administrative staff in Puerto Rico is a genuine challenge. Automation that reduces reliance on staff for repetitive tasks is especially compelling in this context.

**Two-market reality.** Many Puerto Rican business owners serve clients in both Puerto Rico and Orlando (or have offices in both). They need bilingual systems by default. ThunderHouse's bilingual execution is a direct competitive advantage here.

**Government compliance complexity.** CPA and accounting firms in Puerto Rico deal with both federal US and Puerto Rico government portal compliance. This is why the CPA case study (government portal filing automation) resonates strongly in this market.

**Slow adoption, but once adopted, loyal.** PR business owners may take longer to commit, but once they trust a vendor and see results, they tend to be very loyal and refer others actively.

---

## Orlando Market Notes

The Orlando market is secondary but growing for ThunderHouse. The Puerto Rican diaspora in Orlando (especially in the Kissimmee–Orlando corridor) creates a large audience of Spanish-speaking professional service business owners who share Puerto Rico market norms.

- The bilingual positioning works identically
- The WhatsApp channel is relevant
- The same relationship-before-transaction psychology applies
- Orlando buyers may move slightly faster than PR-based buyers due to more competition

---

## What NOT to Do for PR Market

- Don't assume they'll self-serve. The audit call is essential — PR buyers need conversation.
- Don't use overly formal Spanish (e.g., don't avoid common Anglicisms that PR professionals use naturally: "email," "WhatsApp," "follow-up," "intake" are acceptable in Spanish context)
- Don't make the site feel like a mainland US SaaS company that "also speaks Spanish"
- Don't lead with technology. Lead with the business problem.
- Don't create fake urgency ("only 3 audit slots left") — it destroys credibility in a relationship-oriented market

---

## Spanish Copy Guidance

- Use natural Puerto Rico business Spanish, not Castilian Spanish
- Common acceptable Anglicisms: email, WhatsApp, follow-up, intake, workflow, dashboard, app
- Avoid overly bureaucratic phrasing — write like you'd talk in a business meeting
- When in doubt, prefer clarity over formality
- Terms like "captación" (lead capture), "seguimiento" (follow-up), and "automatización" (automation) should be used consistently
- "Consultorio" is better than "oficina médica" for medical practices in PR context
